Volta Greentech at Brilliant Minds 2019

Fredrik Åkerman

2019-06-28

Cows generate ~4% of our global greenhouse gas emissions. That’s 2x more than the world’s fleet of airplanes. And these 4% is just cows farting and burping, nothing else is included. It sounds insane but there are 1.5B cows on the planet.

Bill Gates recently said that if cattle were a country, they would rank third in greenhouse gas emissions. What’s more insane is that this is a source of emissions that have had no solutions. We are about to electrify the transport sector and we know how to produce green electricity, but we have no idea how to reduce the emissions from the cows. To lower the consumption of beef and dairy products is a good and obvious solution. But at scale that transition in consumer behavior will take more time than we have.

I have become obsessed with this challenge and together with Leo Wezelius and Angelo Demeter, we are building up a company around making cows fart and burp less methane. And at Volta Greentech we will do that by feeding cows seaweed.

By adding a tiny amount of a special type of red seaweed called Asparagopsis to the cow’s diet, their enteric methane emissions can be reduced by up to 80%. The picture below is one portion that each cow are supplemented each day.

How it works in simple terms is that the seaweeds bioactive compounds trigger a process in the cow’s digestion system that block the production of methane. And this without affecting the cow’s health. It almost sounds too good to be true, but it builds on many years of research by leading researchers at universities in Australia and in the United States.

Volta Greentech is working on how to get this out to cows at scale.

There are two main parts to this challenge. First, we need to develop economic incentives for the farmers to use this feed supplement to reduce emissions. And then we need to figure out how to produce very large amounts of this seaweed to meet the demand. This specific seaweed has never been commercially produced anywhere in the world before. Exciting progress is being made on both parts.

Thank you Brilliant Minds Foundation for giving us the chance to share what we are working on at Brilliant Minds 2019 in front of such a crowd. The more people that get involved the faster we can make something really impactful.
